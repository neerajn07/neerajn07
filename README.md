## About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Geological Sciences at Queen's University, specializing in computational geophysics and UAV-based radiometric and magnetic surveys.

My research combines physics-based modelling, numerical simulation, inversion, and geophysical data processing. I investigate how survey design, acquisition conditions, detector characteristics, and environmental effects influence data quality and geological interpretation.

I am particularly interested in developing practical computational tools that improve the reliability, resolution, and interpretation of UAV geophysical surveys.

---

## Research Interests

- UAV gamma-ray spectrometry and magnetic surveying
- Forward modelling and three-dimensional inversion
- Survey design and acquisition optimization
- Radiometric data processing and uncertainty analysis
- Geophysical simulation and numerical methods
- Machine learning for lithological mapping
- Integration of magnetic, radiometric, and geological datasets

---

## Current Research

- Developing **RadSIMU**, a physics-based simulation framework for UAV gamma-ray spectrometry
- Building gravity and magnetic inversion workflows using Python and MATLAB
- Investigating the effects of flight altitude, line spacing, detector footprint, and sampling density
- Evaluating counting statistics and acquisition-related noise in radiometric datasets
- Applying machine learning methods to radiometric and magnetic data for lithological classification
- Developing reproducible workflows for UAV geophysical data processing and interpretation
